NARRATIVE STATISTICS FOR OCR EVALUATION CHAPTER
================================================================================

OVERALL PERFORMANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OlmOCRv1 achieved the lowest character error rate (CER=0.041) and highest word accuracy (92.3%), demonstrating superior OCR quality for historical documents.

Supporting Data:
  - best_system: OlmOCRv1
  - best_cer: 0.0410
  - best_wer: 0.0774
  - best_char_accuracy: 0.9590
  - best_word_accuracy: 0.9226
  - worst_system: EasyOCR
  - worst_cer: 0.3822
  - performance_gap: 0.3411


OLMOCR PERFORMANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
olmOCRv2 achieved a character error rate of 0.044, performing within 7.9% of the best system (OlmOCRv1) while being free and open-source.

Supporting Data:
  - olmocr_cer: 0.0443
  - olmocr_wer: 0.0749
  - olmocr_bleu: 0.8856
  - relative_to_best: +7.9%


ERROR TYPE DISTRIBUTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Character errors were dominated by deletions (68.2%), with substitutions comprising 15.6% of errors, indicating that OCR systems primarily struggle with deletions rather than character misrecognition.

Supporting Data:
  - insertion_pct: 16.28%
  - deletion_pct: 68.16%
  - substitution_pct: 15.57%
  - dominant_error_type: deletions


HALLUCINATION RATES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hallucination rates (words not present in the original) averaged 4.08% across all systems, with OlmOCRv2 showing the lowest rate (1.34%) and EasyOCR the highest (8.87%).

Supporting Data:
  - mean_hallucination: 0.0408
  - lowest_system: OlmOCRv2
  - lowest_rate: 0.0134
  - highest_system: EasyOCR
  - highest_rate: 0.0887


BLEU SCORES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BLEU scores averaged 0.678 across systems, with OlmOCRv2 achieving the highest score (0.886), indicating strong n-gram overlap with the gold standard.

Supporting Data:
  - mean_bleu: 0.6781
  - best_system: OlmOCRv2
  - best_bleu: 0.8856


DOCUMENT AGE IMPACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Document age significantly impacted OCR performance: CER increased by 63.3% for 17th-century documents (0.049) compared to 19th-century texts (0.134), reflecting the challenges of archaic typography and deteriorated source materials.

Supporting Data:
  - oldest_group: 1600-1624
  - oldest_cer: 0.0492
  - newest_group: 1800-1824
  - newest_cer: 0.1341
  - degradation_pct: 63.3%


OLMOCR VS TESSERACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
olmOCRv2 (CER=0.044) outperformed Tesseract (CER=0.150) by 70.5%, demonstrating the value of deep learning-based OCR for historical texts.

Supporting Data:
  - olmocr_cer: 0.0443
  - tesseract_cer: 0.1501
  - improvement_pct: 70.5%
