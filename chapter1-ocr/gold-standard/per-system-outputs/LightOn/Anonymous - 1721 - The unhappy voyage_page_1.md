(593) # the unhappy voyage. giving an account of the royal anne galley, captain willis commander, which was split to pieces on the stag rocks on the lizard the 16th of november, 1721, having on board the lord belhaven, who was going as governor to barbados, with several other persons of diffinction, the whole number on board being 210, out of which there were only three saved; whose names are george hain, william goffroy, and thomas lawrence, a boy. to the tune of, womens work is never done, &c. ![image](image_1.png) o’!! the sad and dismal story; brought from off the lizard rocks; the royal anne in all her glory, there receiv’d a fatal shock: the winds and waves did both confpire; for to ruin all therein: oh belhaven! brave belhaven! most unhappy half throu been. governor unto barbados, he was falling bold and brave; little dreaming it was his fortune; for to have a warty grave; with many more brave noble persons, which were put in place of truth, burch! their sands were just expiring, their time was come and die they must. above two hundred souls were perif’d, in an infant all were lost. by furious winds and waves were driven, on the stag rocks they were cast; oh! the difmal cries and scriekings, twould have pierc’d a heart of stone, when the galley split to pieces, for to hear their pitious moan. of all that were on board the vessel, only three could reach the shore, who on a wreck by the winds were carried, thro’ the waves and billows rear; then praise the lord for his great mercy, you that thus have been prefer’d, adore and magnifie his goodness, willingly your maker serve. captain willis was commander, at this most unhappy time, sevage to be attorney general, with many more just in their prime, where going over for to fettle, at barbados we do hear. but by the ocean they were follow’d, as they did their courtes fleer. what a scene of difmal horror, there was seen when this was o’er, bodies floating on the ocean, by the waves were drove on shore, and the country people running, striving who should get the moff, sprinting all without diffinction, tis the custom of the coalf. one gentleman was drove on shore, bout whom they found a thousand pound, whose name’s supposed to be grofter, by writings in h’ pockets found: likewise they say the lord belhaven, having on a diamond ring, his shirt marked b. the floating ocean, did to shore his body bring. oh! the grief and sad diffraction, which this accident hath brought, on the wives and near relations, of the perfus that are lost; some for husbands, other fathers, brothers, kinfmen, do lament, with aking hearts, and weeping eyes, to think how from them they are rent. but now observe the difmal story, given by these three that’s fav’d, george hain, likewise william godfrey, and thomas lawrence, a boy ’tis said, as to barbados we were failing, lord belhaven, he did say, come noble boys and jovial sailors, happy may our voyage be. but drawing near unto the lizard, there the storm began to rife, nothing then but defolation, did appear before our eyes, such a dreadful scene of sorrow, sure before was never seen, oh belhaven! brave belhaven! most unhappy half throu been. captain willis cry’d brave sailors, do your belt, or die we must, lord have mercy, now relieve us, in thy providence we must. save us in the time of danger, for death before our eyes appear, and when they found all hope were over, in tears they all fell down to prayer. then against the fatal rocks, the royal galley split in twain, two hundred souls aloud were crying, lord fave us from the warty main, to see each for his life a shifting, as the waves toft mountains high, the galley in the deep a sinking, while dying groans did pierce the sky. i see the noble lord belhaven, climbing to the top-matt high, with many more of birth and breeding, who likewise in the waves did die, at length the galley split in pieces, death put an end to all their woe, we three poor souls upon a wreck, with waves was settled too and fro. at length unto the shore was driven, where the people took us in: we praised the lord for his great mercy, since we thus prefer’d have been, and fav’d us in the fatal hour, when the waves did us surround, and a many souls did perish, and before our eyes were drown’d: oh! the dreadful grief and sorrow, that their wives and children bear, wives a weeping for their husbands, the children for their father dear, let each render heart implore them, and pity the sorrow they are in, and think upon the lord belhaven, how unhappy he has been. a noble lady in the city, hearing of her husband’s death, twould have mov’d a heart of pity, to hear the words she did express, crying, o my tender jewel, is a warty wave thy tomb, i’ll search the deep to find my dear, o cruel death come seize me soon. thus the raves in the city, in her bed with cords she’s bound, crying night and day my jewel, he is in the ocean drown’d. thus many a mournful widow, and babies fatherless are made, confider then the many dangers, which poor sailor thro’ do wade. many more that night did perish, in the storm as we do hear, as in the downs the fleet was riding, some lord belhaven’s fate did share; lord keep us from all such diffalter, deliver us from fidden death, and give us grace that we may serve, the mighty god while we have breath. early english books online, copyright © 2019 proquest llc images reproduced by courtesy of british library
