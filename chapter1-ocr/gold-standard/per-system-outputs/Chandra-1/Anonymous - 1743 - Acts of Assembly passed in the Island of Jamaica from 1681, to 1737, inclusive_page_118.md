and between *port royal* aforesaid and *passage fort*, the like sum of four ryalls; and between *kingston* aforesaid and *passage fort*, the sum of fix ryalls; and from *port royal* or *kingston* to the several places following, the hire and fare of such wherries with two oars shall be as follows, viz. to *st. james's fort*, five shillings; to *bull bay*, ten shillings; to *yallows*, twenty shillings; to *morant*, or *port morant*, forty shillings; to *old harbour*, *pig bay*, the *bogne*, or *salt river*, one pound, and so in proportion; that is to say, one half more than the prices respectively, as aforesaid, for wherries with four oars. how long wherrymen are to ply. vi. and be it further enacted by the authority aforesaid, that such wherrymen, as aforesaid, shall be, and are hereby obliged to ply from break of day to eight o'clock at night, under the penalty (if he be a white man) that he shall forfeit ten shillings; and if he be a negro, he shall suffer thirty one lashes, to be ordered respectively by any magistrate, before whom complaint shall be made. owners of wharfs to keep scales for the weighing of goods. vii. and be it further enacted by the authority aforesaid, that weights and scales shall be kept by all owners or persons employed for the keeping of publick wharfs, barkadiers, bays, and landing-places in this island, to weigh, if required, all sugar, indigo, piemento, cotton, and other plantation goods, which shall come to such wharf, bay, barkadier, or landing-place, and the weight thereof respectively shall be entered into a book at each place to be kept for that purpose; and for such weighing and entering into such books, as aforesaid, they shall be paid fifteen pence for every hoghead, and seven pence halfpenny for every draught, be it more or less, of other goods of all sorts which shall be so weighed and entered, as aforesaid: and all wharfingers and other persons which shall receive any benefit at any such wharf, barkadier, or landing-place, shall give, or take care that receipts be given for all goods which shall be delivered to them by any person demanding the same, such goods having been first weighed, as aforesaid: and whoſoever shall offend, either by neglecting to procure such scales and weights, or in not weighing the goods delivered to them, and entering the same in a book, as aforesaid, or not giving such receipts, as aforesaid, upon demand; every person so offending, shall, for every such offence, forfeit the sum of twenty pounds; to be recovered in the supreme court of judicature of this island, wherein no effoin, protection, or wager of law shall be allowed, or *non vult ulterius prosequi* be entered; any law, custom, or usage to the contrary notwithstanding. coach hire, viii. and be it further enacted by the authority aforesaid, that the owners or proprietors of any hackney coach or coaches, chariots, and four wheel chaises, which shall ply upon the road for hire, shall have one white man for each such hackney coach or coaches, chariot, or chaise which shall so ply for hire; and the hire and fare shall be as follows, viz. for each coach to and from *passage fort* to *spanish town* and back again, that carries four passengers, thirty shillings; for each coach that carries three passengers, five and twenty shillings; and for two passengers, or for one passenger in any such hackney coach, chariot, or chaise, twenty shillings, and no more. and cart hire. ix. and be it further enacted by the authority aforesaid, that the owners of all carts or waines that shall carry goods for hire, shall be obliged to keep one white man for the first cart or waine, and one white man more for every two such carts or waines they shall keep besides the first, to go forwards and backwards with such carts and waines; and that the fare and hire for such cart and waine shall be as follows, viz. for each hoghead of sugar, five shillings; for each barrel of beef, flour, herrings, or mackerel, the sum of one shilling and three pence, and so in proportion for carriage of other goods from *spanish town* to *passage fort*, and no more. owners of hackney coaches, &c. to give upon oath an account of the number of coaches by them kept, and the drivers names. x. and the owner of each such hackney coach, chariot, or chaise, as aforesaid, and of each cart and waine, or some person for him or her, shall give in an account upon oath at each session of the number of hackney coaches, chariots, chaises, carts, and waines which they keep respectively, and of the names of the white man or men particularly kept for each hackney coach, chariot, chaise, cart or carts, waine or waines;
