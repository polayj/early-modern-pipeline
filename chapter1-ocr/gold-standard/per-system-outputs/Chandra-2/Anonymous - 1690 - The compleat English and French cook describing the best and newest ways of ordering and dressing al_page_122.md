when it is thoroughly boiled, (which will require a considerable time) take it up and pluck off the skin, then take broth you have boiled some fresh meat in, and boil it over again, with vinegar, salt, a good handfull of sweet herbs, ginger, pepper, a good piece of horfe-raddish root scraped, and a quarter of a pound of french-barley; being boiled, strain them through a bag, and souce the head in it; you may serve it up up either in slices, or whole with the horns on. *sides of lamb souced.* bone your side of lamb, soak it and squeeze it well from the blood, wipe it dry, and season the inside with beaten nutmeg, ginger, some sweet herbs minced small, whole coriander-feed, minced lemon-pill and salt; lastly, some slices of broad lard over all: then rowl it up in a coller, and tie it up in a clean cloth, put it into your liquor when it boils, and scum it very well, and then put in sliced ginger, sliced nutmeg, salt, fennel and parsley roots;
