and if he be a negro, he shall suffer thirty one lashes, to be ordered respectively by any magistrate, before whom complaint shall be made. vii. and be it further enacted by the authority aforesaid, that weights and scales shall be kept by all owners or persons employed for the keeping of publick wharfs, barkadiers, bays, and landing-places in this island, to weigh, if required, all sugar, indigo, pimento, cotton, and other plantation goods, which shall come to such wharf, bay, barkadier, or landing-place, and the weight thereof respectively shall be entered into a book at each place to be kept for that purpose; and for such weighing and entering into such books, as aforesaid, they shall be paid fifteen pence for every hoghead, and seven pence halfpenny for every draught, be it more or less, of other goods of all sorts which shall be so weighed and entered, as aforesaid: and all wharfingers and other persons which shall receive any benefit at any such wharf, barkadier, or landing-place, shall give, or take care that receipts be given for all goods which shall be delivered to them by any person demanding the same, such goods having been first weighed, as aforesaid: and whoever shall offend, either by neglecting to procure such scales and weights, or in not weighing the goods delivered to them, and entering the same in a book, as aforesaid, or not giving such receipts, as aforesaid, upon demand; every person so offending, shall, for every such offence, forfeit the sum of twenty pounds; to be recovered in the supreme court of judicature of this island, wherein no eftion, protection, or wager of law shall be allowed, or non cuit ulterius prosequi be entered;
