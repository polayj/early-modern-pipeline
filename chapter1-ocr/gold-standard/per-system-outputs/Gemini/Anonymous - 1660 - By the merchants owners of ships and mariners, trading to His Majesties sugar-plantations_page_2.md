15 by the merchants owners of ships and mariners. trading to his majesties sugar-plantations. it is humbly offered to the conſideration of this honourable houſe, that the white sugar planters, by an accompt untruly ſtated, delivered by them to the committee of the houſe of lords, they doe aſſert that unpurged brown sugars doe produce cleare of charges to the merchant above nineteen ſhillings and nine pence each hundred weight, whereas it is moſst true, and is ready to be made appeare by exaſt accompts, of the sale of that ſort of sugars which came from barbadoes, to the hands of ſeverall merchants of good credit the laſt year, that they doe not produce above eleven ſhillings a hundred, and ſome not above ten shillings; and the planters them-ſelves in their ſaid accompt, rating white sugars but at fifty ſhillings per hundred, which is ten shillings under their juſt and indifferent value, do make them to pro-duce thirty nine shillings per hundred, which is near four for one; and it is rea-dy to be made appear, that ſeveral parcels of white barbadoes sugars have been lately ſold at seventy four and seventy ſix shillings per hundred by an eminent white sugar planter, at which trice they produce above five to one. and it is farther humbly propoſed, that if it ſhall pleaſe this houſe to reduce the rate of unpurged brown sugar to half a farthing per pound, his majeſty will there-by receive a greater and more certain revenue, then by impoſing one farthing per pound on the ſaid sugars, foraſmuch as at a half farthing per pound, all or the greateſt part of the unpurged sugars, that ſhall be imported from his majeſties plan-tations will be refined, and conſumed in this kingdom, and his majeſtie will re-ceive the full exciſe thereof, as wel as of whites; when as paying one farthing per per pound the refiner cannot be encouraged to manufacture them, and being for noe other uſe, they muſt be exported to forreign markets, whereby his majeſtie will not onely loſe the whole duty of exciſe, but alſo half of the cuſtomes inwards, which will be returnable upon the exportation, and ſo the manufacture will be wholly loſt to this kingdom; and forreign nations will reape the sole benefit thereof.
