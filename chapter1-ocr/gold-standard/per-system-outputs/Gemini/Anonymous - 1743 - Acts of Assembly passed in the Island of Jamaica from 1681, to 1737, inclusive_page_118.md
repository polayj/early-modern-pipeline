and if he be a negro, he ſhall ſuffer thirty one laſhes, to be ordered reſpectively by any magiſtrate, before whom complaint ſhall be made. owners of wharfs to keep scales for the weighing of goods. vii. and be it further enacted by the authority aforeſaid, that weights and scales duly ſtampt, ſhall be kept by all owners or perſons employed for the keeping of publick wharfs, barkadiers, bays, and landing-places in this iſland, to weigh, if required, all sugar, indigo, piemento, cotton, and other plantation goods, which ſhall come to ſuch wharf, barkadier, or landing-place, and the weight thereof reſpectively ſhall be entered into a book at each place to be kept for that purpoſe; and for ſuch weighing and entring into ſuch books, as aforeſaid, they ſhall be paid fifteen pence for every hogſhead, and ſeven pence halfpeny for every draught, be it more or leſs, of other goods of all sorts which ſhall be ſo weighed and entered, as aforeſaid: and all wharfingers and other perſons which ſhall receive any benefit at any ſuch wharf, barkadier, or landing-place, ſhall give, or take care that receipts be given for all goods which ſhall be delivered to them by any perſon demanding the ſame, ſuch goods having been firſt weighed, as aforeſaid: and whoſoever ſhall offend, either by neglecting to procure ſuch scales and weights, or in not weighing the goods delivered to them, and entering the ſame in a book, as aforeſaid, or not giving ſuch receipts, as aforeſaid, upon demand, every perſon ſo offending, ſhall, for every ſuch offence, forfeit the sum of twenty pounds;
