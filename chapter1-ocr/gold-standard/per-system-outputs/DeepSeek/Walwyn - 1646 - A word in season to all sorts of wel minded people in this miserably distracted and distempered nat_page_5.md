## (6) this authority, in the worth of all former times, when the strongest force and power was upon them, ever struck closest to the interest of the people, nor did the people in the worth of times, ever fortake them, but maintained their power and privileges, their essence and authority, whenever they called upon them for help and affiance, nor hath this just and powerful authority been more true to the common that chose them, then to those worthy lords and patriots, that at any time have affixed them for the common good of the nation, preferring their honours with a true affection as the liberties of the people; no man can name the time that (intentional-ly) this authority ever did injury to any just interest either at home or abroad, but have borne and suffered much, from those that have made an ill use of their lenity and credulity. all which is necessary to be remembered, and feriously considered in this instant of time, because if the things be feriously laid to heart, it may happily expel those notorious vapors, with which our ayre begins to be infected, wee have ageneration of for- ## (7) getfull, ingrateful people, who because the parliament cannot yeeld unto all they defire (without extreme thraldom to the people, in things ecclesiastical and civil) are degenerate into a malevolent disposition, murmuring and repining at all their proceedings, and making hard continuations of their just endeavours; and by politigue and fubtill means, labour to alinate the hearts of their friends from them; and to incline them to give care to other counsels, laying open their infirmities, (which they should rather go backward to cover) and would ( if they could) poffeffe the world that there is a fort of men that would fettle religion more purely, performe and interpret the covenant more exactly, and doe juftice more;
